The bug was caused by the lack of a boundary check for the `stepSize` variable during integration, allowing it to exceed the target time `t` in both forward and backward directions. This led to the integration process potentially overshooting `t`, causing incorrect computations or failures in applications requiring precise numerical solutions. Evidence for this issue was provided by the `testTooLargeFirstStep` unit test, which failed when the integration time exceeded the valid range, highlighting the problem with step size calculation. The bug was resolved by introducing a conditional adjustment to `stepSize`, ensuring it was reduced to exactly reach `t` in forward integration or adjusted to avoid undershooting `t` in backward integration, thereby maintaining the integration within valid bounds.
