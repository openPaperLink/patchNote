The bug in the `StringUtils.containsAny` method was caused by its failure to correctly handle supplementary characters, which are represented as surrogate pairs in UTF-16, leading to incorrect comparisons of individual `char` values. This resulted in logical errors, as the method could incorrectly return `true` or `false when checking for the presence of supplementary characters in a string. Unit tests, such as `testContainsAnyStringWithSupplementaryChars`, demonstrated the issue by showing incorrect results for surrogate pairs while correctly handling characters in the Basic Multilingual Plane. The patch resolved the bug by adding logic to detect high surrogates and verify valid surrogate pairs, ensuring accurate handling of supplementary characters.
