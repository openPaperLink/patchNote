The bug in the `FastDateFormat` class was caused by incorrect logic in handling the 'y' (year) pattern, where token lengths less than 4 were mistakenly assigned the `TwoDigitYearField.INSTANCE` rule instead of using the `selectNumberRule` method for proper formatting. This led to incorrect formatting of year patterns with token lengths less than 4, such as "yyy" being formatted as a two-digit year instead of a three-digit or full year. Evidence for this issue comes from the `testFormat` unit test, which validates that `FastDateFormat` produces output consistent with `SimpleDateFormat`, and the patch corrects the logic to align with expected formatting rules. The bug was resolved by ensuring that the `TwoDigitYearField.INSTANCE` rule is applied only for token lengths of exactly 2, while the `selectNumberRule` method is used for token lengths less than 4 but not equal to 2, with a minimum token length of 4.
