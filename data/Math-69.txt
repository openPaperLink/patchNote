The bug was caused by an incorrect formula for calculating the two-tailed p-value in Pearson's correlation, where `2 * (1 - tDistribution.cumulativeProbability(t))` was used instead of the correct approach. This led to erroneous p-values, especially for correlation coefficients near 1 or -1, potentially misleading users with invalid statistical conclusions. Evidence of the issue is demonstrated by the unit test `testPValueNearZero`, which fails for edge cases due to the flawed formula. The bug was resolved by replacing the formula with `2 * tDistribution.cumulativeProbability(-t)`, ensuring accurate computation of the two-tailed p-value across all correlation values.
