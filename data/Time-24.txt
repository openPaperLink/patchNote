The bug in the `computeMillis` method of `DateTimeParserBucket` was caused by improper handling of the `resetFields` flag, leading to inconsistent results when parsing overlapping or conflicting date fields, such as week-year and month-week combinations. Its impact was incorrect parsing results for certain date formats, particularly those involving week-based calculations, resulting in mismatched dates as evidenced by failing unit tests like `testParseLocalDate_weekyear_month_week_2011`, which expected `LocalDate(2011,1,3)` but returned incorrect values. The evidence supporting this analysis includes multiple failing tests across different years, highlighting a systemic issue with field resetting logic during parsing. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional block to the `computeMillis` method to ensure proper resetting of fields in the correct order, using a modified reset condition (`i == (count - 1)`), thereby fixing the parsing inconsistencies.
