The bug was caused by an overly restrictive conditional check that required both `options.dependencyOptions.needsManagement()` and `options.closurePass` to be true, unnecessarily skipping dependency management when `closurePass` was not enabled. This led to improper sorting and management of JavaScript dependencies, breaking code execution in scenarios relying on `goog.provide` and `goog.require`. Evidence from the `needsManagement` method in `DependencyOptions` and the `testDependencySorting` unit test confirmed that dependency management should occur independently of `closurePass`. The resolution involved simplifying the conditional logic by removing the `options.closurePass` check, ensuring dependency management is performed whenever required.
