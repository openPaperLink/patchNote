The bug was caused by an incorrect condition that checked if the traversal was in the global scope (`t.inGlobalScope()`) instead of verifying if the parent node had associated JSDoc information (`parent.getJSDocInfo() != null`). This led to false negatives in detecting invalid overrides of private properties, allowing violations of access control rules to go unnoticed. Evidence for this issue is demonstrated by the failing unit tests `testNoPrivateAccessForProperties6` and `testNoPrivateAccessForProperties8`, which expected errors like `BAD_PRIVATE_PROPERTY_ACCESS` and `PRIVATE_OVERRIDE` but did not receive them due to the faulty condition. The bug was resolved by replacing the incorrect condition with a check for JSDoc information on the parent node, ensuring proper identification of property overrides and restoring the intended access control behavior.
