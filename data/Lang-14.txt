The bug in the `StringUtils.equals` method was caused by the incorrect assumption that the `equals` method of `CharSequence` always compares the content of sequences, which is not true for non-`String` implementations like `StringBuilder` or custom `CharSequence` types. This led to incorrect equality results, causing potential logic errors in applications relying on this method for content comparison. Evidence from the `testEquals` unit test showed inconsistencies, such as successful comparisons between a `String` and a `StringBuilder` with the same content but failures with custom `CharSequence` implementations. The issue was resolved by explicitly handling `String` instances with their `equals` method and using `CharSequenceUtils.regionMatches` for other `CharSequence` types to ensure consistent, character-by-character content comparison.
