The bug was caused by incorrect handling of time zone transitions during overlaps from daylight saving time changes, where the logic failed to consider valid offset adjustments from the previous transition. This led to the `getOffsetFromLocal` method returning incorrect offsets during overlaps, resulting in inaccurate local time calculations, particularly in regions like Moscow during daylight saving transitions. Evidence for this issue comes from unit tests such as `test_getOffsetFromLocal_Moscow_Autumn`, which highlighted cases where the expected offset was not correctly computed. The bug was resolved by adding logic to check the validity of the previous transition and adjusting the offset accordingly, ensuring the correct offset is returned during overlaps.
