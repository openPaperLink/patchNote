The bug in `EmbeddedRungeKuttaIntegrator` arises from improper initialization and conditional reassignment of the `scale` array, leading to potential misuse of tolerance vectors. This can result in incorrect step size computations during integration of ordinary differential equations, affecting error estimation and potentially causing inaccurate numerical solutions. Evidence is found in the `polynomial` test, where the bug disrupts expected evaluation counts due to its sensitivity to the `scale` used for step size calculation. The fix involves consistently initializing the `scale` array to match `y0.length` and correctly computing scalar and vector tolerances for each element, ensuring consistent behavior during integration.
