The bug in the `MathUtils` class's `equals` method arises from its simplistic comparison logic, which does not account for precision errors inherent in floating-point arithmetic. Consequences include incorrect evaluations in scenarios where precision comparisons are crucial, such as numerical computations, leading to problems in test scenarios expecting `True` for near-equal floating-point numbers. Evidence of this issue is found in the `testArrayEquals`, where `Double.NaN` values are not treated as equal, resulting in failed logical comparisons. The fix involves refactoring the `equals` method to use an overloaded version with a `maxUlps` argument, enabling tolerance in floating-point comparisons by setting a default tolerance level. This approach ensures numbers are identified as equal not only when precisely the same but also when infinitesimally close within a defined range of ULPs, aligning with common practices in numerical algorithms.
