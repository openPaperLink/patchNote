The bug in the `escapeRegex` method was caused by flawed logic in the handling of consecutive whitespace characters, where the `wasWhite` variable failed to properly account for multiple spaces, leading to incorrect regex generation. As a result, the `FastDateParser` was unable to correctly parse date patterns containing consecutive whitespace, potentially causing parsing errors or runtime issues. Evidence for this issue comes from the failing unit test `testLANG_831`, which demonstrates incorrect parsing of the pattern `"M E"` with the input `"3  Tue"` due to the faulty regex. The bug was resolved by removing the `wasWhite` logic and simplifying the handling of whitespace, ensuring all whitespace characters are consistently replaced with the regex pattern `\\s*+`, thereby fixing the parsing issue.
