The bug in the `getPct(Object v)` method was caused by an incorrect delegation to `getCumPct(Comparable<?> v)` instead of the intended `getPct(Comparable<?> v)`, leading to the method returning cumulative percentages rather than the percentage of values equal to the input. This resulted in incorrect frequency calculations, potentially causing logical errors in applications relying on the deprecated method for accurate results. Evidence for this issue is found in the `testPcts` unit test, where assertions like `assertEquals("one pct", 0.25, f.getPct(1), tolerance)` failed due to the method returning cumulative percentages instead of the expected percentage. The bug was resolved by updating the method to correctly delegate to `getPct((Comparable<?>) v)`, ensuring it now returns the intended percentage of values equal to the input.
