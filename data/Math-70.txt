The bug in the `BisectionSolver` class was caused by an incorrect method signature, where the `solve` method omitted the crucial `UnivariateRealFunction f` argument, necessary for evaluating function values during the bisection method. This omission led to the method improperly functioning, as it couldn't evaluate whether a root existed within the specified interval, rendering it unusable for finding zeros of univariate functions. Evidence of this issue was highlighted by the failing `testMath369` unit test, which aimed to find the root of a sine function within specific bounds. The fix involved modifying the `solve` method to correctly pass the function `f` along with the `min` and `max` parameters, restoring the method's ability to perform accurate root-finding calculations.
