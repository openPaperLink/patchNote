The bug was caused by the absence of validation for the range of bounds, leading to an overflow when the difference between the upper and lower bounds exceeded the maximum representable value for a `double`. This overflow disrupted the optimization process, resulting in undefined behavior, incorrect results, or program crashes, making the optimizer unreliable for certain input ranges. Evidence for this issue is demonstrated by the unit test `testBoundaryRangeTooLarge`, which triggers the bug using bounds that exceed the safe range for `double` values. The patch resolves the bug by introducing a validation step that checks for infinite differences between bounds and throws a `NumberIsTooLargeException` with detailed context, preventing further execution and ensuring clear error reporting.
