The bug arises when constructing a date for February 29 in a non-leap year under the Gregorian calendar, as the method `getDateTimeMillis` throws an `IllegalFieldValueException` without accounting for the Julian calendar's leap year rules during the Gregorian cutover. This leads to application failures when handling historical dates around the cutover period, breaking functionality for users relying on accurate date processing in this range. Evidence for the bug is provided by the failing unit test `testLeapYearRulesConstruction`, which attempts to create a `DateMidnight` object for February 29, 1500—a date valid in the Julian calendar but invalid in the Gregorian calendar. The patch resolves the issue by introducing a `try-catch` block that retries with February 28 for invalid February 29 dates, suppressing the exception if the date is valid under the Julian calendar and before the Gregorian cutover, or rethrowing it otherwise.
