The bug was caused by the `squareRoot` method always using `EigenDecomposition` to compute the square root of the weight matrix, which is inefficient for diagonal matrices that can be processed more directly. This led to significant performance degradation, particularly in scenarios with large datasets or repeated calls, such as the `testLargeSample` unit test involving 40,000 observed points. Evidence for this issue comes from the performance bottleneck observed in the test, where the unnecessary use of `EigenDecomposition` slowed down computations. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional check to detect diagonal matrices (`m instanceof DiagonalMatrix`) and directly computing the square root of their diagonal entries using `FastMath.sqrt`, thereby improving efficiency.
