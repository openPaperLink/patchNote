The bug in the `repairAndDecode` method was caused by a missing condition to check whether the `isRepairMode` flag was enabled, resulting in the repair logic being bypassed even when boundaries were defined and repair mode was active. This led to incorrect behavior where input variables were not adjusted to fit within the specified boundaries, potentially producing invalid or suboptimal optimization results. Evidence for this issue was provided by the `testMath864` unit test, which failed before the patch because the method did not enforce boundary constraints, causing the optimization result to exceed the upper boundary. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional check to ensure that the repair logic is applied only when both `boundaries` are defined and `isRepairMode` is `true`, thereby properly adjusting input variables before decoding.
