The bug in the `Fraction` class's `reduce` method was caused by its failure to properly handle cases where the numerator was zero, as it did not return the canonical `Fraction.ZERO` instance when the denominator was not one. This led to inconsistent behavior, where zero-valued fractions with different denominators (e.g., `0/100`) were not reduced to `Fraction.ZERO`, causing incorrect equality checks and unexpected results in operations. Evidence for this issue came from the `testReduce` unit test, which showed that `Fraction.getFraction(0, 100).reduce()` did not return the expected `Fraction.ZERO` instance, failing the assertion `assertSame(result, Fraction.ZERO)`. The bug was resolved by adding a check at the start of the `reduce` method to return `Fraction.ZERO` whenever the numerator is zero, ensuring consistent representation of zero-valued fractions.
