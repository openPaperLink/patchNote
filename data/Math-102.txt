The bug was caused by the lack of a mechanism to handle significant differences between the total sums of observed and expected counts, leading to incorrect chi-square statistic calculations when these totals were misaligned. This resulted in inaccurate statistical results and potentially incorrect conclusions in tests relying on this method, particularly in cases with large discrepancies between observed and expected counts. Evidence for this issue includes unit tests like `testChiSquareLargeTestStatistic`, which highlight the problem and demonstrate the need for a rescaling mechanism. The bug was resolved by introducing logic to compute the sums of observed and expected counts, detect significant differences, and apply a rescaling factor (`ratio`) to adjust the expected counts, ensuring accurate and consistent chi-square calculations.
