The bug was caused by the use of `Double.doubleToRawLongBits` for comparing the `real` and `imaginary` parts of `Complex` objects, which failed to account for floating-point quirks like signed zero and NaN handling. As a result, the `equals` method could incorrectly determine two numerically identical `Complex` objects as unequal, potentially leading to erroneous behavior in code relying on equality checks. Evidence for this issue comes from the failing unit test `testMath221`, which demonstrated the flawed equality evaluation during a multiplication operation. The bug was resolved by replacing the bit-level comparison with a direct numerical comparison using the `==` operator, ensuring equality is based on actual numerical values.
