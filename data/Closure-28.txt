The bug in the `InlineCostEstimator` class was caused by the `addConstant` method being improperly implemented to add a hardcoded value `"0"` instead of dynamically processing constants through the `add` method. This resulted in constant values like `true`, `false`, and `null` not being correctly folded, leading to inaccurate cost estimation and potentially inefficient or malformed generated code. Evidence for this includes the failure of the `testIssue728` test, which showed incorrect folding of constants like `false`, and the `testCost` test, which revealed discrepancies in cost calculations. The issue was resolved by overriding the `addConstant` method to delegate constant handling to the `add` method, ensuring proper dynamic processing and alignment with the intended behavior.
