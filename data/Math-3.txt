The bug was caused by the lack of special case handling for single-element arrays in the `linearCombination` method, leading it to perform unnecessary computations intended for multi-element arrays instead of directly calculating the scalar product. This resulted in inefficiencies and potential inaccuracies, as redundant operations like initializing `prodHigh` and computing `prodLowSum` were irrelevant for single-element arrays. Evidence for this analysis comes from the unit test `testLinearCombinationWithSingleElementArray`, which confirms the method's behavior when handling single-element arrays. The issue was resolved by adding a conditional check for `len == 1`, allowing the method to directly return the product of the first elements of `a` and `b`, bypassing unnecessary computations.
