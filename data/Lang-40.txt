The bug in the `containsIgnoreCase` method was caused by the use of `String.toUpperCase` for case-insensitive comparison, which is locale-dependent and can produce incorrect results in locales like Turkish, where the uppercase conversion of "i" is not "I". This led to incorrect behavior of the method in non-default locales, particularly those with special case-mapping rules, such as failing to recognize "i" and "I" as equivalent in the Turkish locale. Evidence for this issue comes from the unit test `testContainsIgnoreCase_LocaleIndependence`, which tests the method across multiple locales, including Turkish, using cases like "i" vs. "I" and locale-sensitive Greek sigma characters. The bug was resolved by replacing the locale-dependent `toUpperCase` approach with the locale-independent `String.regionMatches` method, ensuring consistent case-insensitive comparisons across all locales.
