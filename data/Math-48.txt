The bug in the `REGULA_FALSI` algorithm arises because it fails to handle cases where the computed value of `x` remains equal to `x1` across consecutive iterations, causing the algorithm to get stuck. This leads to wasted computational resources and misleads users by continuing iterations until the maximum limit is reached, without making progress toward convergence. Evidence for this issue is demonstrated in the `testIssue631` test case, where the solver fails to converge due to the repeated equality of `x` and `x1`. The patch resolves the bug by introducing a check that throws a `ConvergenceException` when `x` equals `x1`, effectively terminating the process and preventing unnecessary iterations.
