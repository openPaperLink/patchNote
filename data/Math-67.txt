The bug in the `MultiStartUnivariateRealOptimizer` class arises from incorrect retrieval of the optimization result and function value, as the original methods `getResult` and `getFunctionValue` fetch results directly from the underlying `optimizer`. This approach only reflects the last optimization run instead of utilizing the `optima` and `optimaValues` arrays that cache multiple starts, undermining the multi-start capability. Consequently, results are unreliable for processes requiring evaluation across multiple runs, as potentially optimal solutions from earlier runs may be overlooked. Evidence of this issue is illustrated in the `testQuinticMin`, where expected results fail to match actual outcomes due to this bug. The fix involves modifying `getResult` and `getFunctionValue` to return `optima[0]` and `optimaValues[0]`, ensuring result accuracy by reflecting the best solutions found across multiple optimization starts.
