The bug in `FastDateParser` arises because it does not validate whether the entire `pattern` string has been fully processed, allowing unprocessed characters to be silently ignored and leading to incorrect or incomplete parsing behavior. This can cause unexpected parsing failures or incorrect results, particularly for patterns with unmatched or extraneous characters, such as `"d'd"` or `"d'd'"`. Evidence of the issue is demonstrated in the unit test `testLANG_832`, which shows the parser failing to handle incomplete patterns correctly and producing incorrect results. The patch resolves the bug by adding a validation step that checks if the `patternMatcher` has fully processed the `pattern`, throwing an `IllegalArgumentException` when unprocessed characters remain, ensuring only valid patterns are accepted.
