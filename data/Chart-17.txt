The bug in the `TimeSeries` class was caused by the `clone` method incorrectly using `createCopy`, which only copied a subset of the `data` list instead of performing a deep clone, leading to incomplete cloning. As a result, the cloned `TimeSeries` object shared references to the original `data` list, causing unintended side effects where changes to one object affected the other, violating expected cloning behavior. Evidence of this issue is demonstrated in the unit test `testBug1832432`, where adding a new data item to the original series after cloning caused the test to fail, showing that the `data` list was not properly cloned. The patch resolved the bug by replacing `createCopy` with `ObjectUtilities.deepClone` on the `data` list after invoking `super.clone`, ensuring the cloned object has a deep copy of the `data` list and remains independent of the original.
