The bug was caused by the incorrect use of the `allResultsMatch` method instead of `anyResultsMatch` in the `mayBeString` function when the `recurse` parameter is true, leading to overly strict evaluations of whether a node may represent a string. As a result, certain expressions were incorrectly evaluated as not possibly being strings, potentially causing incorrect optimizations or transformations in the JavaScript compiler and breaking the intended behavior of the compiled code. Evidence for this comes from the failing unit test `testIssue821`, which involved expressions with conditional (`? :`) and logical (`||`) operators that required only one branch to satisfy the condition. The bug was resolved by replacing `allResultsMatch` with `anyResultsMatch`, ensuring the function correctly identifies nodes that may represent strings if any possible result matches the predicate.
