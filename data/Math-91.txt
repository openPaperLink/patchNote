The bug in the `compareTo` method was caused by the use of `doubleValue()` for fraction comparison, which introduced precision loss when handling large numerators and denominators. This led to incorrect results in comparisons, impacting functionality that relied on accurate ordering or equality checks, as demonstrated in the `testCompareTo` unit test where fractions `pi1` and `pi2` were inconsistently compared due to precision issues. Evidence supporting this analysis includes the small tolerance difference (`1.0e-20`) between their `doubleValue()` results, which still caused the method to incorrectly indicate one fraction as greater than the other. The bug was resolved by replacing `doubleValue()` with exact arithmetic using `long` multiplication of numerators and denominators, ensuring precise and reliable comparisons.
