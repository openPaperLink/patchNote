The bug was caused by the calculation of the `widthRange` parameter in the `RectangleConstraint` object for the `rightBlock` arrangement, where `new Range(0.0, constraint.getWidth() - w[2])` could result in a negative upper bound if `constraint.getWidth()` was less than `w[2]`, violating the `Range` class's requirement for valid bounds. This issue could lead to an `IllegalArgumentException` during runtime, disrupting the layout arrangement process and potentially causing application crashes or rendering failures. Evidence for this analysis comes from the `Range` constructor's validation logic and the unit test `testSizingWithWidthConstraint`, which indirectly exposed the invalid range calculation. The bug was resolved by updating the `widthRange` calculation to use `Math.max(constraint.getWidth() - w[2], 0.0)`, ensuring the upper bound is non-negative and preventing the creation of invalid ranges.
