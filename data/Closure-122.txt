The bug was caused by overly simplistic logic that detected suspicious block comments resembling JSDoc annotations by checking for specific substrings (`"/* @"` or `"\n * @"`), failing to account for variations in formatting or whitespace. This led to inconsistent behavior where certain valid JSDoc-like comments were not flagged as suspicious, reducing the effectiveness of the warning mechanism. Evidence for this comes from unit tests like `testSuspiciousBlockCommentWarning5` and `testSuspiciousBlockCommentWarning4`, which show scenarios where the original implementation failed to trigger warnings for comments with varying whitespace and formatting. The issue was resolved by replacing the substring checks with a more robust regular expression (`Pattern.compile("(/|(\n[ \t]*))\\*[ \t]*@[a-zA-Z]")`), ensuring all JSDoc-like comments are properly identified and flagged.
