The bug was caused by the code's failure to distinguish between unnamed function expressions (valid) and unnamed function statements (invalid), leading to improper handling of unnamed function statements. This resulted in invalid unnamed function statements, such as `function() {};`, being incorrectly parsed without raising an error, potentially causing issues in the transformation of the new AST to the old AST. Evidence for this issue is demonstrated in the `testUnnamedFunctionStatement` test case, which shows that such invalid statements do not trigger errors. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional check to verify the `functionType` of the `functionNode` and reporting an error if it is not a `FunctionNode.FUNCTION_EXPRESSION`, ensuring proper validation of unnamed functions.
