The bug was caused by declaring the variable `n1n2prod` as an `int`, which led to integer overflow when calculating the product of the sample sizes `n1` and `n2` for large datasets. This overflow resulted in incorrect computation of the expected value (`EU`) in the Mann-Whitney U test's normal approximation, potentially producing invalid statistical results and misleading users. Evidence for this issue comes from the `testBigDataSet` unit test, where arrays of size 1500 caused the product (2,250,000) to exceed the maximum value of an `int`, leading to test failures. The bug was resolved by changing the type of `n1n2prod` from `int` to `double`, ensuring accurate calculations without overflow.
