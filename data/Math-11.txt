The bug was caused by an incorrect calculation in the density function of the `MultivariateNormalDistribution` class, where the expression `-dim / 2` used integer division, truncating the result to an integer for odd dimensions and leading to an incorrect exponent. This resulted in inaccurate density values, potentially affecting applications like statistical modeling, machine learning, and simulations that rely on this distribution. The issue was identified through the failing `testUnivariateDistribution` unit test, which revealed a mismatch in density values between univariate and single-dimensional multivariate normal distributions. The bug was resolved by replacing `-dim / 2` with `-0.5 * dim`, ensuring the calculation is performed as a floating-point operation and preserving the correct exponent.
