The bug was in `cumulativeProbability` method of the `NormalDistributionImpl` class and was caused by `MaxIterationsExceededException` handling of edge cases which can't be calulated in alllowed max iterations. This will lead to exception throwing when FastMath.abs(dev) is bigger than 20*standardDeviation, which may make application crashed. The evidence is in the failing test case `testExtremeValues`, which showed in cases where big values like `Double.MAX_VALUE` and `Double.NEGATIVE_INFINITY` can't be processed correctly.  The bug was resolved by introducing a condition that firstly check the value of variables to process, if the absolute difference value between it and mean value, the method directly returns 0.0 or 1.0 to avoid exception handling.
