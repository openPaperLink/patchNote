The bug in the `greatestCommonDivisor` method was caused by its failure to handle edge cases where one of the input integers (`u` or `v`) is `Integer.MIN_VALUE`, which overflows when negated, leading to incorrect calculations or infinite loops. This impacted operations involving fractions with `Integer.MIN_VALUE` in their numerator or denominator, resulting in incorrect results or runtime exceptions in methods like `reduce` and `getReducedFraction`. Evidence for this issue was observed in unit tests such as `Fraction.getReducedFraction(-2, Integer.MIN_VALUE)` and `Fraction.getReducedFraction(-7, Integer.MIN_VALUE)`, which produced incorrect outputs or unexpected exceptions. The bug was resolved by adding a check to detect `Integer.MIN_VALUE` and throw an `ArithmeticException` to prevent overflow, along with adjustments to handle zero values correctly.
