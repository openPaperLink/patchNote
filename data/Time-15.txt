The bug was caused by the lack of a check for the edge case where `val1` equals `Long.MIN_VALUE` and `val2` equals `-1`, leading to an arithmetic overflow when multiplying these values. This resulted in the `safeMultiply` method producing incorrect results or undefined behavior, potentially causing runtime errors in applications relying on this utility. Evidence for this issue comes from the unit test `testSafeMultiplyLongInt`, which demonstrated that the method failed to throw the expected `ArithmeticException` for the problematic case `FieldUtils.safeMultiply(Long.MIN_VALUE, -1)`. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional check in the `case -1` block to throw an `ArithmeticException` with a descriptive message when this edge case occurs, ensuring proper handling and prevention of overflow.
