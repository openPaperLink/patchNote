The bug in the `SimplexSolver` class was caused by an incorrect comparison condition, where `MathUtils.compareTo(entry, 0, epsilon) >= 0` allowed zero entries to be considered valid for ratio calculation, leading to potential division by zero, which is mathematically invalid in the Simplex method. This caused the algorithm to produce incorrect results or fail during optimization, impacting its ability to solve certain linear programming problems, as demonstrated by the failure of the `testMath288` unit test. The test provided evidence of the issue by defining constraints and an objective function that exposed the incorrect handling of zero entries in the tableau. The bug was resolved by changing the condition to `MathUtils.compareTo(entry, 0, epsilon) > 0`, ensuring only positive entries are used for ratio calculation and aligning the implementation with the mathematical requirements of the Simplex method.
