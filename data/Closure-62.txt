The bug in the `LightweightMessageFormatter` class was caused by an incorrect boundary condition, where the validation `0 <= charno && charno < sourceExcerpt.length()` failed to account for cases when `charno` equaled `sourceExcerpt.length()`. This led to formatting errors in error messages, particularly when the error occurred at the end of a line, making it difficult for developers to pinpoint the issue. Unit tests such as `testFormatErrorSpaceEndOfLine1` and `testFormatErrorSpaceEndOfLine2` highlighted the problem, as the formatter failed to properly display a caret (`^`) for errors at the line's end. The patch resolved the issue by updating the condition to `charno <= sourceExcerpt.length()`, ensuring accurate error highlighting even for errors at the end of a line.
