The bug was caused by the absence of a validation check to ensure that the function values at the interval endpoints (`yMin` and `yMax`) have opposite signs, a necessary condition for bracketing in the Brent algorithm. As a result, the solver could attempt to find a root in a non-bracketing interval, potentially leading to runtime errors, infinite loops, or incorrect results. Evidence for this issue comes from the unit test `testBadEndpoints`, which demonstrated that an `IllegalArgumentException` was not raised when the interval endpoints failed to bracket a root. The bug was resolved by adding a validation check to ensure `yMin * yMax <= 0`, throwing an `IllegalArgumentException` with a descriptive error message if the condition is violated, thereby preventing the solver from proceeding with invalid intervals.
