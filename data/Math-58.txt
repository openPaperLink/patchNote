The bug was caused by the redundant invocation of the `fit` method in the `GaussianFitter` class, where a new instance of `Gaussian.Parametric` was unnecessarily passed, despite the method internally handling the creation of the parametric function. This redundancy could lead to inefficiencies and inconsistencies in curve fitting, potentially affecting the accuracy of the fitted parameters and downstream computations. Evidence for this issue comes from the `testMath519` unit test, which validates the correctness of the fitted parameters using a predefined dataset and highlights the need for simplification in the method's invocation. The bug was resolved by modifying the `fit` method to use only the guessed parameters (`guess`), removing the redundant creation of `Gaussian.Parametric`, thereby improving clarity, maintainability, and ensuring the method behaves as intended.
