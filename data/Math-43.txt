The bug in the `SummaryStatistics` class was caused by incorrect `instanceof` checks in the `addValue` method, which failed to properly handle cases where statistical calculation implementations (`meanImpl`, `varianceImpl`, `geoMeanImpl`) were overridden with instances of the same class but with different configurations. This resulted in overridden implementations not being updated when new values were added, leading to incorrect results for methods like `getMean`, `getVariance`, and `getGeometricMean`, particularly when custom configurations were applied. Evidence for this issue came from failing unit tests, such as `testOverrideMeanWithMathClass`, `testOverrideGeoMeanWithMathClass`, and `testOverrideVarianceWithMathClass`, which demonstrated that the overridden implementations were not functioning as expected. The bug was resolved by replacing the `instanceof` checks with direct equality checks (`!=`) to ensure that overridden implementations are always updated, regardless of their class type.
