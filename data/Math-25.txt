The bug is caused by the variable `c2` being zero in certain ill-conditioned cases, leading to a division by zero during the calculation of amplitude (`a`) and pulsation (`omega`) in the harmonic fitting process. This results in undefined behavior or runtime exceptions, causing the harmonic fitting to fail, particularly in edge cases like those demonstrated in the test case `testMath844`. Evidence supporting this analysis includes the initialization of variables (`sxy`, `sxz`, `sx2`, `syz`) to zero, which can lead to `c2` being zero, and the patch explicitly referencing this scenario (`cf. MATH-844`). The bug is resolved by introducing a conditional check for `c2 == 0` and throwing a `MathIllegalStateException` with a localized error message (`LocalizedFormats.ZERO_DENOMINATOR`), preventing invalid calculations and providing clear feedback to the user.
