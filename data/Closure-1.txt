The bug in the `RemoveUnusedVars` class was caused by the unconditional removal of unused function arguments, even when the `removeGlobals` flag was set to `false`, which contradicted the intended behavior. This resulted in the incorrect removal of function arguments during compilation, violating the expectations of developers who configured `removeGlobals=false` to preserve unused arguments. Evidence for this issue was found in the failing unit tests `testRemoveGlobal2`, `testRemoveGlobal1`, and `testRemoveGlobal3`, which demonstrated that unused arguments were removed despite the flag being disabled. The bug was resolved by adding a conditional check to ensure that the function exits early without removing unused arguments when `removeGlobals` is set to `false`, thereby aligning the behavior with the configuration.
