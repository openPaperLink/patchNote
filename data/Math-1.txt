The bug was caused by a missing condition in the overflow-checking logic during fraction conversion, where the loop failed to terminate early when `epsilon` was `0.0` and the absolute value of `q1` was less than `maxDenominator`. This led to unnecessary `FractionConversionException` errors, preventing valid fractions from being created in edge cases with strict constraints (`epsilon = 0.0`) and small `maxDenominator`. Unit tests, such as `testDigitLimitConstructor`, highlighted the issue, as constructing fractions like `0.4` with a small `maxDenominator` (e.g., `9`) resulted in exceptions instead of valid outputs. The patch resolved the bug by adding a condition to break the loop early under these circumstances, ensuring consistent and correct behavior in both the `BigFraction` and `Fraction` classes.
