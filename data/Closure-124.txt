The bug was caused by the incorrect traversal of `node` objects in the original code, which failed to handle deeply nested `GETPROP` chains, resulting in incomplete processing of the object hierarchy. This led to the optimization logic skipping valid optimizations or producing incorrect results when chaining assignments involving nested `GETPROP` properties. Evidence for this issue is demonstrated in the unit test `testIssue1017`, where repeated assignments like `x.parentNode.parentNode` were not handled properly due to the flawed traversal logic. The bug was resolved by introducing a `while` loop to iteratively traverse the `node` hierarchy until a non-`GETPROP` node is reached, ensuring correct processing of all nested properties.
