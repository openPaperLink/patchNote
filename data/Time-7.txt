The bug was caused by calculating the default year using the `Chronology` object derived from the offset-adjusted local time (`instantLocal`), instead of the UTC-based instant, leading to incorrect year calculations during date parsing. This resulted in parsing errors near year boundaries (e.g., December 31st or January 1st) in different time zones, where the time zone offset affected the year value. Evidence of the issue is demonstrated by the failing unit tests `testParseInto_monthDay_feb29_newYork_startOfYear` and `testParseInto_monthDay_feb29_tokyo_endOfYear`, which incorrectly parsed February 29th due to the flawed default year calculation. The patch resolved the issue by using the `Chronology` derived directly from the UTC-based instant to calculate the default year, ensuring consistent and correct parsing regardless of time zone offsets.
