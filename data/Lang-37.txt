The bug in the `ArrayUtils.addAll` method was caused by attempting to merge two arrays with incompatible component types, such as `Integer[]` and `Long[]`, which led to an `ArrayStoreException` during the `System.arraycopy` operation due to JVM type enforcement. Its impact was that developers encountered runtime exceptions without clear explanations, making debugging difficult and obscuring the root cause of the incompatibility. Evidence for this issue comes from the unit test `testJira567`, which demonstrated the failure when merging incompatible arrays, expecting an `IllegalArgumentException` but encountering an `ArrayStoreException` instead. The bug was resolved by adding a `try-catch` block to check type compatibility using `type1.isAssignableFrom(type2)` and throwing an `IllegalArgumentException` with a descriptive message for incompatible types, while rethrowing the original exception for other cases.
