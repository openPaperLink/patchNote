The bug was caused by the absence of null checks for the `dataset` variable in two methods of the `PiePlot` class, leading to a `NullPointerException` when the `dataset` was null. This issue impacted the application by causing runtime exceptions during operations like rendering a pie chart with no data, preventing the chart from being drawn and disrupting functionality. Evidence for this analysis is provided by the failing unit test `testDrawWithNullDataset`, which attempts to render a pie chart with a null dataset and encounters the exception. The bug was resolved by adding null checks in the affected methods, ensuring safe execution by returning default values (`0.0` or skipping calculations) when the `dataset` is null.
