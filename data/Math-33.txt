The bug was caused by the use of the `maxUlps` parameter in the `Precision.compareTo` method for floating-point comparisons in the `SimplexTableau` class, which is unsuitable for checking optimality in the Simplex algorithm; the `epsilon` parameter, representing absolute error tolerance, should have been used instead. This led to incorrect identification of columns to drop during optimization, causing the algorithm to produce suboptimal results or fail to solve certain linear programming problems, as evidenced by the failure of the `testMath781` unit test. The test case highlighted the need for absolute error-based comparisons, and the use of `maxUlps` was inconsistent with the algorithm's requirements and the test's expectations. The bug was resolved by replacing `maxUlps` with `epsilon` in the `Precision.compareTo` method, ensuring proper floating-point comparisons aligned with the algorithm's intended behavior.
